# Email Drafting & Outreach Templates
**Save as:** `templates/05-email-outreach.md`
**Purpose:** Professional email templates for every situation — cold outreach, follow-up, client communication, and more.

---

## MY EMAIL VOICE
- Tone: [e.g., warm and professional / direct and brief / friendly and human]
- Length: [e.g., under 150 words per email]
- Sign-off: [e.g., Best, / Thanks, / Regards,]
- I never open with: [e.g., "I hope this email finds you well"]

---

## TEMPLATE 1: Cold Outreach

Subject: [Specific, personal, not salesy]

Hi [First name],

[1 sentence showing you know something specific about them or their work.]

[1-2 sentences on why you are reaching out. Be direct about what you want.]

[1 sentence on what is in it for them — the value to them, not you.]

[Clear, low-friction ask — one specific thing, not multiple.]

[Sign-off]
[Your name]

---

## TEMPLATE 2: Follow-Up (No Response)

Subject: Re: [Original subject]

Hi [First name],

Following up on my note from [X days ago].

[One new piece of value or reason to respond — not just "checking in."]

[Same ask, restated in fewer words.]

[Sign-off]
[Your name]

---

## TEMPLATE 3: Partnership or Collaboration Pitch

Subject: Collaboration idea — [specific topic]

Hi [First name],

[Genuine compliment about their work — specific, not generic.]

I work with [describe your audience or platform]. [One sentence on what you have built.]

Idea: [Describe the collaboration in 1-2 sentences. What each person does.]

Why this works for both of us: [1 sentence each on mutual benefit.]

Open to a quick call this week or next if this sounds interesting?

[Sign-off]
[Your name]

---

## TEMPLATE 4: Delivering Work to a Client

Subject: [Project name] — ready for review

Hi [First name],

[Deliverable name] is ready. Find it here: [link or attachment]

Key decisions made:
- [Decision 1 and why]
- [Decision 2 and why]

What I need from you:
- [Specific input or approval needed, if any]

Feedback requested by: [date]

[Sign-off]
[Your name]

---

## TEMPLATE 5: Thank You After a Call

Subject: Great talking today

Hi [First name],

Thanks for the time today. Key things from our conversation:

- [Point 1 discussed]
- [Point 2 discussed]
- [Action item — who does what by when]

[One genuine sentence about what you are looking forward to.]

[Sign-off]
[Your name]

---

## EMAIL RULES
- Subject line must make the recipient want to open it
- First sentence must not be about you
- State the ask clearly — do not bury it
- One ask per email, never two
- Read it aloud before sending — if it sounds robotic, rewrite it
- Never send an email you would not want forwarded to someone else
