# Research & Analysis Framework
**Save as:** `templates/03-research-framework.md`
**Purpose:** Structures every research task so Claude delivers thorough, honest, well-sourced output.

---

## SOURCE HIERARCHY
Trust sources in this order:
1. Peer-reviewed academic papers
2. Official government or institutional reports
3. Reputable journalism with editorial standards
4. Industry reports from known research firms
5. Expert opinion with clear credentials
6. Community consensus and practitioner knowledge

Always note which type of source information comes from.

**What the Evidence Says**
What does the weight of evidence suggest? Where is there consensus? Where is there genuine debate?

**What Experts Disagree On**
Be honest about uncertainty. Flag where evidence is thin or contested.

**Practical Implications**
Given this research, what should I actually do or think differently?

**What to Read Next**
3-5 specific resources worth consulting.

## MY RESEARCH RULES
- Never fabricate citations. If you reference a study, flag it so I can verify it.
- Always note: sample size, methodology, and limitations for any study cited.
- Distinguish clearly between correlation and causation.
- When I paste raw notes, clean them without adding information I did not include.
- Flag any claim that seems contested in the literature.

## AI PROMPTS FOR RESEARCH
- "Research [topic] using the framework in this file. Answer: [specific question]."
- "I have these notes on [topic]: [paste notes]. What am I missing? What should I look into next?"
- "What are the most common misconceptions about [topic] and what does the evidence actually say?"
- "Compare [Position A] vs [Position B] on [topic]. Where is there genuine disagreement among experts?"
